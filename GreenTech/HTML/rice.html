Fertilizer Requirements

Rice requires a balanced supply of nutrients for optimal growth. A common recommendation is a complete fertilizer with an NPK ratio of 15-15-15 or 10-20-10. Apply phosphorus and potassium at planting, and nitrogen should be applied in split doses during the growing season.

Expected Diseases

Rice is prone to several diseases including:

  • Rice Blast: Caused by the fungus Magnaporthe oryzae.
  • Leaf Blight: Caused by the bacterium Xanthomonas oryzae.
  • Brown Spot: Caused by the fungus Bipolaris oryzae.
  • Sheath Blight: Caused by the fungus Rhizoctonia solani.

Required Diagnosis

Diagnosis for rice health includes:

  • Leaf Analysis: To check for nutrient deficiencies or toxicities.
  • Soil Testing: To ensure soil nutrient levels and pH are within the optimal range.
  • Pest and Disease Monitoring: Regular inspection for symptoms of pests and diseases.

About

Rice (Oryza sativa) is a staple food crop and one of the most important cereal grains. It is primarily grown in warm, tropical, and subtropical regions. Rice requires a lot of water for its cultivation and is used for food, animal feed, and various industrial purposes.

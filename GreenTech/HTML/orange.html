Fertilizer Requirements

Oranges benefit from a balanced fertilizer with an NPK ratio of 8-8-8 or 10-10-10. During the growing season, apply a high-potassium fertilizer to promote fruit development. Ensure the soil has adequate magnesium and calcium for optimal fruit quality.

Expected Diseases

Oranges are susceptible to several diseases, including:

  • Citrus Canker: A bacterial disease causing lesions on leaves, stems, and fruit.
  • Citrus Greening: A bacterial disease leading to yellowing of leaves and fruit drop.
  • Powdery Mildew: A fungal disease producing white, powdery spots on leaves and fruit.
  • Root Rot: Caused by soil-borne fungi, affecting the root system and causing decline in tree health.

Required Diagnosis

Diagnose orange tree issues by:

  • Visual Inspection: Look for signs of disease, pests, and nutrient deficiencies on leaves and fruit.
  • Soil Testing: Check soil pH and nutrient levels to ensure they meet the requirements for healthy growth.
  • Leaf Analysis: Helps in identifying nutrient imbalances and specific deficiencies affecting the tree.

About

Oranges (Citrus sinensis) are one of the most widely consumed fruits globally, known for their juicy and tangy flavor. They are grown in subtropical and tropical regions and are a major source of vitamin C. Orange trees require well-drained soil and protection from frost. They are typically harvested once the fruit reaches maturity and has developed its characteristic color.

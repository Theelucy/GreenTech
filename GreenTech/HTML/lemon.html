Fertilizer Requirements

Lemons thrive with a balanced fertilizer. An NPK ratio of 10-10-10 is recommended for young trees, while mature trees benefit from a higher potassium formula such as 6-6-12. Apply the fertilizer every 6-8 weeks during the growing season. Ensure the soil is rich in magnesium, which can be supplemented with Epsom salts if needed.

Expected Diseases

Lemons are susceptible to several diseases, including:

  • Citrus Canker: A bacterial infection causing lesions on leaves, stems, and fruit.
  • Citrus Greening: A bacterial disease that causes yellowing of leaves and premature fruit drop.
  • Powdery Mildew: A fungal disease producing a white, powdery coating on leaves and stems.
  • Root Rot: Caused by waterlogging and poor soil drainage, leading to root decay and tree decline.
More Information

Required Temp, pH, Humidity

Temperature: Lemons prefer temperatures between 50°F and 85°F (10°C to 29°C). They are sensitive to frost and should be protected from temperatures below 30°F (-1°C).

pH: Lemons grow best in slightly acidic to neutral soil with a pH range of 5.5 to 6.5.

Humidity: Lemons require moderate humidity. Excessive humidity can lead to fungal diseases, so ensure good air circulation around the trees.

Required Diagnosis

Diagnose lemon tree issues by:

  • Visual Inspection: Regularly check for signs of disease, pests, and nutrient deficiencies on leaves and fruit.
  • Soil Testing: Test soil pH and nutrient levels to ensure they are within the optimal range for lemon growth.
  • Leaf Analysis: Helps identify specific nutrient deficiencies or imbalances affecting the tree's health.

About

Lemons (Citrus limon) are valued for their tart flavor and high vitamin C content. They are grown in subtropical and tropical regions and require well-drained soil and protection from frost. Lemon trees typically start producing fruit 6-9 months after flowering, with the fruit maturing in 6-9 months. Regular pruning helps maintain tree health and encourages fruit production.

Seasonal Produce

Winter

  • Kale: Hardy and can withstand frost, perfect for winter gardens.
  • Spinach: Cold-tolerant and grows well in winter.
  • Brussels Sprouts: Grows well in cold weather, harvesting into the winter months.
  • Carrots: Can be grown and harvested in winter.
  • Garlic: Plant in late autumn or early winter for a summer harvest.

Summer

  • Tomatoes: Thrive in warm temperatures and need plenty of sun.
  • Cucumbers: Heat-loving and grow rapidly in summer.
  • Peppers: Prefer hot weather and lots of sunlight.
  • Zucchini: Grows abundantly in summer.
  • Eggplant: Requires warm soil and long days of sunlight.

Autumn

  • Broccoli: Can be planted in late summer for an autumn harvest.
  • Cauliflower: Thrives in cooler weather, making it ideal for autumn.
  • Pumpkins: Usually harvested in autumn, perfect for the season.
  • Beets: Grow well in the cooler temperatures of autumn.
  • Swiss Chard: Tolerates frost and can be harvested into late autumn.

Spring

  • Lettuce: Grows quickly in the cooler temperatures of early spring.
  • Peas: Prefers the cool weather of spring for optimal growth.
  • Radishes: One of the fastest-growing crops in spring.
  • Asparagus: A perennial that begins to grow in early spring.
  • Carrots: Can be planted in early spring for a summer harvest.

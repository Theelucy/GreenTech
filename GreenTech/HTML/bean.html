Fertilizer Requirements

Beans require a balanced supply of nutrients for optimal growth. A common recommendation is to use a fertilizer with an NPK ratio of 10-20-10 or 15-15-15. Beans generally need less nitrogen compared to other crops, so ensure phosphorus and potassium are well-supplied to support flowering and pod development.

Expected Diseases

Beans can be affected by several diseases, including:

  • Bean Rust: Caused by the fungus Uromyces appendiculatus, resulting in reddish-brown pustules on leaves.
  • Bean Mosaic Virus: Leads to mottled or mosaic patterns on leaves, affecting plant growth.
  • Anthracnose: Caused by the fungus Colletotrichum lindemuthianum, resulting in dark lesions on leaves, stems, and pods.
  • White Mold: Caused by the fungus Sclerotinia sclerotiorum, which can cause rot in the stems and pods.

pH: Beans prefer a soil pH between 6.0 and 7.0. Soils that are too acidic or alkaline can hinder nutrient availability.

Humidity: Ideal relative humidity is around 50% to 70%. Excessive humidity can increase the risk of fungal diseases.

Required Diagnosis

For diagnosing beans, consider:

  • Leaf Analysis: To check for nutrient deficiencies or imbalances.
  • Soil Testing: To assess soil nutrient levels and pH.
  • Regular Inspection: For early detection of diseases and pests, and to monitor plant health.

About

Beans (Phaseolus vulgaris) are an important legume crop known for their high protein content. They grow best in well-drained soils and are often used as a cover crop to improve soil fertility. Beans are sensitive to frost and require proper disease management to ensure high yields.

Fertilizer Requirements

Watermelons require a balanced supply of nutrients for optimal growth. A recommended fertilizer is one with an NPK ratio of 10-10-10 or 15-15-15. Apply a higher amount of potassium to support fruit development. It's also beneficial to use a pre-planting fertilizer that includes a mix of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium.

Expected Diseases

Watermelons can be susceptible to several diseases, including:

  • Powdery Mildew: Characterized by white, powdery spots on leaves and stems.
  • Downy Mildew: Causes yellowing and death of leaves, with a fuzzy gray or white mold on the undersides.
  • Anthracnose: Results in dark, sunken lesions on fruit, stems, and leaves.
  • Watermelon Mosaic Virus: Leads to mottling and distortion of leaves, stunted growth, and poor fruit development.

pH: Watermelons prefer a soil pH between 6.0 and 7.0. Well-drained soils with this pH range promote healthy root development.

Humidity: Ideal relative humidity is around 60% to 70%. High humidity can increase the risk of fungal diseases.

Required Diagnosis

For diagnosing watermelons, consider:

  • Leaf Analysis: To check for nutrient deficiencies or excesses.
  • Soil Testing: To ensure proper nutrient levels and pH balance.
  • Regular Monitoring: For early detection of diseases and pests, and to monitor plant growth and fruit development.

About

Watermelons (Citrullus lanatus) are a popular fruit known for their high water content and refreshing taste. They are grown in warm climates and require ample sunlight and well-drained soil. Watermelons have a long growing season and benefit from consistent watering and nutrient management.
